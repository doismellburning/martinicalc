Martini Calculator

According to Kingsley Amis's Everyday Drinking, a Dry Martini should be made with "12 to 15 parts gin [and] 1 part dry vermouth".

There would be a calculator here, but having done the maths, it just didn't feel very..."Kingsley" to be mucking around with sub-mililitre measurements of vermouth, when realistically my friends and I make martinis my taking gin, and applying "some" vermouth. So here's some calculations with a rough guide, for my benefit and hopefully yours.

How much gin do you have?

A 375ml Bottle!

Use "about a shot" of dry vermouth (25-31.25 ml)

A 750ml Bottle!

Use "about two shots" of dry vermouth (50-62.5 ml)

A 1l Bottle!

Use "about three shots" of dry vermouth (67.5-82.5 ml)
